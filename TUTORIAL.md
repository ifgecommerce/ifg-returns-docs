# Getting started

Three short phases: set up the return form on your storefront, see what a customer experiences, then manage a request from your admin. Most stores are fully set up in under ten minutes.

## 1. Set up the return form

**1. Complete the guided onboarding.**
Open the app and fill in your company's legal details and your return address. These are required: they're what your customers see on their withdrawal confirmation and where returned items get sent, and the app tracks them as part of your compliance status.

**2. Add a return block to your theme.**
Use the "Open Theme Editor" shortcut at the end of onboarding (or go there directly from your Shopify admin). Click *Add block* / *App embeds*, search for **IFG Returns**, and choose the block that fits where you want it:

- `Module` — embeds the full form in a page, like a dedicated "Returns" page.
- `Pop-up` — a button that opens the form in a modal, placed anywhere on a page.
- `Floating` — a floating button visible on every page; turned on from the *App embeds* panel rather than added to a single page.

> You can enable more than one — for example a floating button site-wide plus a dedicated page for a footer link.

**3. Save the theme.**
The app's dashboard automatically detects which block is active — no need to confirm it manually.

## 2. What your customer sees

**1. No account needed.**
Customers open the return form directly from your storefront — no login required. The form's language automatically follows your store's configured language, including full right-to-left layout for Arabic and Hebrew.

**2. Order lookup.**
The customer enters their order number, email, and order date. These are checked live against the real order in Shopify, so only genuine orders can be submitted.

**3. Instant confirmation.**
On submission, the customer gets an immediate confirmation with their case reference — the request now appears in your admin.

## 3. Manage a request

**1. Open "Returns" in the app.**
Every new request appears here with a status badge (pending, approved, refunded, and so on) so you can see what needs attention at a glance.

**2. Review and approve.**
Open a case, check the items and reason, and click **Approve**. Once the returned item arrives, click **Confirm receipt**.

**3. Refund.**
Select the items and amount to refund and confirm. On plans that include direct refunds, the refund is issued immediately from within the app — no need to jump to the separate Shopify order page.
